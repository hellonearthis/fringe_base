CRUSH THE BUG

Someone wakes up. It is a working man. They are a bug! Sounds familiar, doesn't it...

Insects in lab coats, an exterminator's moral crisis, a rude bug, lamp-obsessed moths and phone-life balance: this playful work hops from scene to scene, crafting an absurd odyssey through the minds of two clowns.

CRUSH THE BUG is a new New Zealand clown work from recent Ecole Phillipe Gaulier alumni Daniel Nodder and Sean Burnett Dugdale-Martin after successful development in New Zealand and internationally. These award-winning clowns have concocted an interactive clown show about confusion, metamorphosis, togetherness, and humans' great capacity for passionate distractedness.

Dirty Old Songs

Compelled to prove the past wasn't so prudish, Ms. Weeds will take you on a sonic ride with some of the dirtiest songs from blues history. Uniquely qualified for this task, burlesquer come blues musician, she will perform songs for your pleasure while telling you the stories behind them. In 2024, she co-produced her first ever Fringe show, Burlesque and Blues, the Devil's Own, which was nominated for the NZ Fringe Festival awards for Momentous Movement. Dirty Old Songs will be Ms. Weeds' first ever solo show!

Ms. Weeds is a retro weird singing burlesque darling from Pōneke, Wellington and has been performing around Aotearoa New Zealand since 2011. She's a multi-instrumentalist and with a voice that packs a punch, lovingly referred to as the human fog horn. Winner of numerous awards such as:

You can find her most weekends either backstage or on stage in the various cabaret shows of Pōneke or playing blues music with other local mischief makers.

Driving me crazy

Driving me crazy is a no-brakes comedy collision starring real-life married comedy couple Mo Munn and Q Potts -- and yes, they're still together... somehow.

This fast-paced, unfiltered blend of stand-up and sketch dives head-on into the chaos of co-piloting a life -- and a car -- with your partner. From white-knuckled driving lessons to petty habits that spark full-blown meltdowns, nothing is sacred. Refusing to drive a manual? Refusing to wipe the bench properly? Buckle up, because it's all going under the microscope.

With razor-sharp wit, undeniable chemistry, and just the right amount of road rage, Driving Me Crazy swerves through the hilarious potholes of modern relationships -- where love, frustration, and GPS miscommunications collide.

Expect rapid-fire banter, brutally relatable stories, and sketches that hit a little too close to home. Mo and Q are living proof that marriage is messy, weird, and completely worth mocking on stage.

Perfect for date night, mate night, or when you just need to laugh at someone else's dysfunction for once -- Driving Me Crazy is a comedic car crash you'll be glad you didn't miss this Valentine's weekend!

Mo Munn is a multi-award-winning comedian, recently named Best Newcomer at the 2025 NZ International Comedy Festival. Q Potts is a rising star on the scene, and a 2025 Wellington Raw Comedy Quest Finalist.

Relationships don't come with a manual. But this show? It's the next best thing.

Shift of Tides

The Shift of Tides
Created and performed by Karolina Gorton and Pipi-Ayesha Evans

In the windows of Graphic Comics at 105 Cuba Street.

Step into a powerful physical theatre experience that explores the unpredictable nature of beginnings and endings - fluid, nonlinear, and often beyond our control. The Shift of Tides invites audiences to witness shifting states of emotion and movement, challenging perceptions and embracing the beauty of transformation.


Karolina Gorton and Pipi-Ayesha Evans guide you through the circle of life - from the first breath to the last - navigating the spaces between yesterday and tomorrow, joy and sorrow, presence and absence. It's a journey of memory, resilience, joy, and rediscovery, where we carry our burdens and find the strength to lift them. We are not helpless... are we?


Developed as an incubator performance with support from the Viewfinder window gallery in Whakatū, Nelson, this striking work unfolds across two windows, two spaces - where reality bends through glass and connection is tested. The performers explore the boundaries between these spaces, revealing the tension, intimacy, and distance that shape our human experience.

A visceral, poetic, and visually striking performance that resonates deeply with the rhythms of life and the tides of emotion.

Tap Fusion

Tap Fusion is a high energy, percussive Tap Dance show that fuses with Street Dance, Contemporary Dance and Live Original Music.

Creators Brandon & Cameron Carter-Chan, Director and Co Director of Tap NZ, are some of New Zealand's top Choreographers with Brandon winning the National Tap Dance Championship in 2014.  Brandon & Cameron have taught and choreographed in the dance community winning awards across New Zealand, Australia and the United States for over 10 years.  Their Tap Dance Company Tap NZ focuses on Tap Innovation and has recently been invited to perform in Los Angeles and Melbourne to stage their creative works.

Tap NZ is very proud to present Tap Fusion, a show that champions Tap Dance Innovation. The audience are taken on a journey that begins with the roots of Tap, to a future of endless creative possibilities.

Tap Dance originated in the 19th Century with history dating back to 1840's New York, USA. Rhythm and sound have been the foundation as Tap Dance changed and evolved over the years. Tap Fusion is a show designed to push the boundaries of the Tap Dance discipline as well as acknowledging its foundations of exceptional sound and rhythmical composition.

Inspired by famous shows such as Tap Dogs, Riverdance and STOMP, Tap Fusion is a sound and percussion show at the core, designed to entertain the audience through a multi-layered sensory experience.

Tap Shows are rare in the entertainment world with so few that champion Tap Dance at the forefront of the show. Tap Fusion is a platform that showcases Tap as the leading voice of the story where audiences will experience a fresh perspective on what Tap Dance truly represents.  Music of the Sole.

About The Rebel Alliance

The Rebel Alliance is an independent Auckland based theatre company. Founded in 2006 they have,to date, presented 11 works, 7 of which have been premieres of new New Zealand plays. They also started off the development of Centrepoint which co-won the Adam Award for best new New Zealand play in 2015.

Often solidly planted in real life the works they present prove that truth often surpasses fiction. Extraordinary life stories move, provoke, inspire and put our own lives into perspective. They can anchor us in turbulent times.

In 2024 they premiered their 11th play, The Valentina, as part of the Auckland Arts Festival.

Driving me crazy

Driving me crazy is a no-brakes comedy collision starring real-life married comedy couple Mo Munn and Q Potts -- and yes, they're still together... somehow.

This fast-paced, unfiltered blend of stand-up and sketch dives head-on into the chaos of co-piloting a life -- and a car -- with your partner. From white-knuckled driving lessons to petty habits that spark full-blown meltdowns, nothing is sacred. Refusing to drive a manual? Refusing to wipe the bench properly? Buckle up, because it's all going under the microscope.

With razor-sharp wit, undeniable chemistry, and just the right amount of road rage, Driving Me Crazy swerves through the hilarious potholes of modern relationships -- where love, frustration, and GPS miscommunications collide.

Expect rapid-fire banter, brutally relatable stories, and sketches that hit a little too close to home. Mo and Q are living proof that marriage is messy, weird, and completely worth mocking on stage.

Perfect for date night, mate night, or when you just need to laugh at someone else's dysfunction for once -- Driving Me Crazy is a comedic car crash you'll be glad you didn't miss this Valentine's weekend!

Mo Munn is a multi-award-winning comedian, recently named Best Newcomer at the 2025 NZ International Comedy Festival. Q Potts is a rising star on the scene, and a 2025 Wellington Raw Comedy Quest Finalist.

Relationships don't come with a manual. But this show? It's the next best thing.

Shift of Tides

The Shift of Tides
Created and performed by Karolina Gorton and Pipi-Ayesha Evans

In the windows of Graphic Comics at 105 Cuba Street.

Step into a powerful physical theatre experience that explores the unpredictable nature of beginnings and endings - fluid, nonlinear, and often beyond our control. The Shift of Tides invites audiences to witness shifting states of emotion and movement, challenging perceptions and embracing the beauty of transformation.


Karolina Gorton and Pipi-Ayesha Evans guide you through the circle of life - from the first breath to the last - navigating the spaces between yesterday and tomorrow, joy and sorrow, presence and absence. It's a journey of memory, resilience, joy, and rediscovery, where we carry our burdens and find the strength to lift them. We are not helpless... are we?


Developed as an incubator performance with support from the Viewfinder window gallery in Whakatū, Nelson, this striking work unfolds across two windows, two spaces - where reality bends through glass and connection is tested. The performers explore the boundaries between these spaces, revealing the tension, intimacy, and distance that shape our human experience.

A visceral, poetic, and visually striking performance that resonates deeply with the rhythms of life and the tides of emotion.

Tap Fusion

Tap Fusion is a high energy, percussive Tap Dance show that fuses with Street Dance, Contemporary Dance and Live Original Music.

Creators Brandon & Cameron Carter-Chan, Director and Co Director of Tap NZ, are some of New Zealand's top Choreographers with Brandon winning the National Tap Dance Championship in 2014.  Brandon & Cameron have taught and choreographed in the dance community winning awards across New Zealand, Australia and the United States for over 10 years.  Their Tap Dance Company Tap NZ focuses on Tap Innovation and has recently been invited to perform in Los Angeles and Melbourne to stage their creative works.

Tap NZ is very proud to present Tap Fusion, a show that champions Tap Dance Innovation. The audience are taken on a journey that begins with the roots of Tap, to a future of endless creative possibilities.

Tap Dance originated in the 19th Century with history dating back to 1840's New York, USA. Rhythm and sound have been the foundation as Tap Dance changed and evolved over the years. Tap Fusion is a show designed to push the boundaries of the Tap Dance discipline as well as acknowledging its foundations of exceptional sound and rhythmical composition.

Inspired by famous shows such as Tap Dogs, Riverdance and STOMP, Tap Fusion is a sound and percussion show at the core, designed to entertain the audience through a multi-layered sensory experience.

Tap Shows are rare in the entertainment world with so few that champion Tap Dance at the forefront of the show. Tap Fusion is a platform that showcases Tap as the leading voice of the story where audiences will experience a fresh perspective on what Tap Dance truly represents.  Music of the Sole.

About The Rebel Alliance

The Rebel Alliance is an independent Auckland based theatre company. Founded in 2006 they have,to date, presented 11 works, 7 of which have been premieres of new New Zealand plays. They also started off the development of Centrepoint which co-won the Adam Award for best new New Zealand play in 2015.
